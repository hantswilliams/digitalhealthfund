Health Affairs:Top-Funded Digital Health Companies And Their Impact On High-Burden, High-Cost Conditions

In this article by Safavi and colleagues, the argument is made that we need more scientific studies (peer-reviewed evidence) to support the efficacy of digital health products. A wide variety of well-funded, successful companies exist that offer digital products. Yet, very few of these companies have published studies that support the feasability, acceptability, and or efficacy of their products for non-healthy populations.

In summary, this articles highlights the existing gap - continued need for research to support digital health products with persons that have existing chronic illnesses (real patients).
